I am a postdoctoral researcher with a background in cognitive neuroscience and philosophy. Currently, I work in the Sleep & Cognition Group led by Eus van Someren at the Netherlands Institute for Neuroscience. My broad research interest lies in what determines a conscious experience. As part of my postdoc, I am thinking about how sleep state misperception in insomnia comes about. Why do some patients think they were awake for large parts of the night while objective measures (e.g. EEG recordings) demonstrate regular sleeping patterns?

Previously, I worked on how the brain processes information during sleep at the University of Cambridge and investigated prediction errors in schizophrenia at the UMC Utrecht. During my PhD at the University of Würzburg, I focused on the behavioural and neural mechanisms underlying attentional shifts to humans in our environment.
